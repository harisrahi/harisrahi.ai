01 The Problem

Most companies rent their AI know-how. Their people never build their own.

Here's what I see happening: companies invest in generic AI training, hire consultants to redesign the work in slide decks, then bring in managed services to run the tools. Each step looks sensible. Together they propagate vendor dependence.

AI training that sits outside a team's actual work never sticks. Tools built without the people who use them don't get trusted, and nobody in-house can keep them current once the vendor moves on. AI is changing every few weeks right now. The only way to keep up is to teach your people to think and build with it themselves.

Invest in your people first. That investment compounds as AI advances, and it protects your institutional intelligence.
